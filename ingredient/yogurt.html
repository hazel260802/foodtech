Yogurt

1. Storage

Fridge temperature: Yogurt can be stored in the refrigerator (40°F = 4,4°C) 1 to 2 weeks or frozen (0°F=-17,7°C) for 1 to 2 months.

Room temperature: yogurt should be discarded if left out for more than 2 hours at room temperature.

2. Characteristics

  • Provides: high in protein, calcium, vitamins and live culture, or probiotics , which can enhance the gut microbiota.
  • Shouldn’t go with: sour fruits
  • Signs of expiration: Look for a larger-than-normal amount of liquid on the surface, a curdling texture near the bottom, and any sign of mold.y

3. Recommended dish: Garlic Dill French Fries with Lemon Dill Yogurt Sauce

a. Ingredient

  • 1 ½ lb Russet potatoes
  • 2 tablespoons finely chopped fresh dill, divided
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • ½ teaspoon sea salt

    • Lemon Dill Yogurt Sauce:

  • ¼ cup Greek yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons mayonnaise
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice, freshly squeezed is best
  • 1 tablespoon finely chopped dill
  • 1 small garlic clove, finely minced
  • 1 pinch sea salt, more to taste

b.Instructions

  • Preheat the oven to 425 degrees. Line 2 13"x18" baking sheets with parchment paper.
  • Scrub the potatoes and dry them well. There's no need to peel them. Cut the potatoes into ⅛ inch sticks, place them into a large bowl, add the olive oil, 1 ½ tablespoons of the dill, and the sea salt and toss well to coat.
  • Divide the fries between the two prepared baking sheets. (Don't try to cram them all on one baking sheet or they won't crisp.) Bake in the preheated oven for 20 minutes.
  • While the fries are baking, mix the lemon dill sauce ingredients in a small bowl.
  • Remove the fries from the oven and flip them over. Put them back into the oven until they are cooked through and nicely browned, 5-10 minutes. Once they have finished cooking remove them from the oven and sprinkle the remaining ½ tablespoon of dill over the top. Serve the fries with the lemon dill sauce on the side.
